Disease Information

Lung Model

This model helps identify various types of lung cancers, including:

  • Adenocarcinoma: A type of lung cancer that begins in the cells that line the alveoli.
  • Large Cell Carcinoma: A fast-growing type of lung cancer that can occur in any part of the lung.
  • Squamous Cell Carcinoma: A type of lung cancer that originates in the squamous cells lining the lungs.
  • Normal: No abnormalities detected.

Brain Model

This model classifies brain conditions:

  • 1: Indicates a specific type of abnormality detected.
  • 2: Represents another condition that requires further investigation.
  • 3: Denotes a severe condition that needs immediate attention.

Chest Model

This model helps detect conditions related to the chest:

  • Normal: No issues detected in chest imaging.
  • Pneumonia: An infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs.

Colon/Lung Model

This model identifies both colon and lung conditions:

  • Colon Adenocarcinoma: A type of cancer that forms in the mucus-producing glandular cells.
  • Normal Colon Tissue: No abnormalities in colon tissue.
  • Lung Adenocarcinoma: A type of lung cancer starting in glandular cells.
  • Normal Lung Tissue: No abnormalities in lung tissue.
  • Lung Squamous Cell Carcinoma: A form of lung cancer from squamous cells.

Bone Marrow Model

This model helps detect bone marrow conditions:

  • Benign: Indicates non-cancerous cells in the bone marrow.
  • Malignant Early Pre-B: Early-stage malignant cells in bone marrow.
  • Malignant Pre-B: Intermediate stage malignant cells.
  • Malignant Pro-B: Advanced-stage malignant cells requiring immediate attention.
